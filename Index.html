The Rats of Brooklyn-- why some Neighborhoods have more Rats than others

By:Ashley Borja

Around 3 million rats inhabit New York City. Recently, Mayor Eric Adams's "War on Rats" campaign intensified the media spotlight on rats. Certain neighborhoods across the boroughs have a more prominent presence of rodents.
Sourced from the Noun Project

NYC has named areas with high rat activity Rat Mitigation Zones (RMZs). Information is sourced from complaints by neighborhood residents.

According to data from the Environment and Health Data Portal on nyc.gov, neighborhoods with the most significant rat presence are:

  1. Manhattan: Harlem
  2. Brooklyn: Bed Stuy and Bushwick
  3. Bronx: Grand Concourse
  4. Manhattan: East Village and Chinatown

Complaint Data (2022): Numbers for Rat Sightings:

  • Bed Stuy and Bushwick: 1,509
  • Grand Concourse: 990

    New York City Rats are of the "Norway rat (brown rat) species". It's one of the most common species of rats. The average brown rat is 16 inches long and weighs around one pound. Rats in New York City only need one ounce of food and water a day to survive.

    Sourced from the Noun Project

    Rats seek locations to live that provide what they need to survive: food, water, and shelter. Rats are attracted to heavily populated areas as they have much larger trash output. Garbage is a huge source of food for rats. According to the New York City Department of Health, to combat rats moving into trash cans, members of New York City should place "trash in covered metal or heavy-duty plastic trash containers."

    It is easy for rats to find shelter in the city, as there are many locations to create borrows. Some rats burrow around trees in the dirt. Building owners should rat-proof locations by removing appliances, junk, old fences, and repairing walls. Landlords must seal any holes that may allow rats to enter their residential buildings.

    Sourced from the Noun Project

    Infestations pose a health risk for NYC residents. Rats can carry pathogens that can cause an illness, especially in young children. Apart from the pathogens, they have Ectoparasites (ex, lice). Rats can spread disease to people or pets who come into contact with rats' bodily substances like saliva and fecal droppings.

    Sourced from the Noun Project

    NYC has proposed and signed various initiatives to combat the rat issue. Most recently, on October 11th, 2023, Mayor Adams and his administration unveiled their plan to containerize all trash in residential buildings. Starting in the fall of 2023, "buildings with nine or fewer residential units will be required to place all trash in secure containers," states nyc.gov.
